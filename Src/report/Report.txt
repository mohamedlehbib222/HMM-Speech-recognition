

HMM Speech Recognition Report

This project implements a simple speech recognition system using Hidden Markov Models (HMM).

Two words are modeled: EV and OKUL.

Each word is represented by a separate HMM model. The models contain transition probabilities and emission probabilities representing the likelihood of observing certain audio features.

Observation symbols:
0 = High frequency
1 = Low frequency

Example observation sequence:
[High, Low]

The likelihood of the observation sequence is calculated for both models. The model with the highest log-likelihood determines the recognized word.

Noise in speech data can affect emission probabilities by making observations less reliable. This can reduce the accuracy of the model.

Modern speech recognition systems often use Deep Learning instead of HMM because deep neural networks can handle large vocabularies and complex speech patterns more effective
